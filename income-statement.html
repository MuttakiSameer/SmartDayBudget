What is an Income Statement?

An income statement (often called a profit and loss statement, or P&L) is one of the core financial reports used to evaluate the economic health of a business. Unlike a balance sheet which represents a single snapshot, the income statement aggregates all revenues generated and expenses incurred over a specific period (e.g., monthly, quarterly, or annually).

How Profits and Losses are Calculated

Our free P&L ledger tracker handles the essential accounting arithmetic dynamically. The fundamental equation governing the ledger is:

Net Income = Revenue − Expenses

If your total revenues exceed your combined operational expenses, the statement shows a Net Profit. If expenses exceed income, the ledger reflects a Net Loss. The system visually tracks these trends instantly, updating the profit and loss badges automatically as you add items.
